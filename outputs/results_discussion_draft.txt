The experimental results demonstrate a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed Hybrid SDR Top-k approach against established durable exact match baselines. From a runtime perspective, both the Full_SSA and Full_PRA exact match baselines exhibited significant latencies heavily correlated with the scale of the user database. However, by introducing the SDR lookup step as an initial approximate candidate filter, the Hybrid_SSA and Hybrid_PRA models reduced runtime latencies dramatically. In our real-world dataset validation, the Full SSA algorithm required approximately ~0.058 seconds, whereas the Hybrid PRA framework answered identical queries in 0.0018 seconds, offering a latency reduction multiplier of approximately ~32x.

This performance increase stems essentially from our model's capacity to aggressively prune irrelevant time series matrices. The algorithms achieved pruning ratios scaling smoothly between 97% to 99.5%, implying that over 97% of the user space was completely disqualified from the expensive geometric exact validations. We also observed robust recall ratios when transitioning from highly synthetic random sequences to dense real-world subsets, showcasing the robustness of employing temporal-aggregated vectors within prefix filters.

In conclusion, our pipeline establishes a fundamentally efficient frontier for Continuous Reverse Top-k queries without necessitating continuous recalculation steps against inactive user vectors.
