SUPERVISOR MEETING SCRIPT (2-3 minutes)
========================================

[INTRO - 15 seconds]

My thesis topic is "Durable Reverse Top-k Queries on Time-Varying
Preferences."  The goal is to efficiently find users who consistently
rank a query item among their least-preferred items across multiple
time windows.

[WHAT I IMPLEMENTED - 30 seconds]

I implemented methods from two base papers:

First, an approximate reverse k-ranks method that uses pre-built rank
tables to quickly estimate item rankings per user.  I validated this on
MovieLens and got a 30x speedup with 98% recall.

Second, two exact durable verification methods called SSA and PRA that
check whether a user qualifies across enough time windows.

Then I combined them into a hybrid pipeline: a fast filter to prune
users, followed by exact verification on the survivors only.

[LEVEL 1 RESULT - 15 seconds]

The static Level 1 validation on MovieLens showed the rank table
approach works well: 30x faster than brute force with 98% accuracy.
This confirmed the foundation is solid.

[LEVEL 2 ISSUE - 30 seconds]

When I moved to the temporal Level 2, the hybrid pipeline had a recall
problem.  The candidate filter was only recovering about 60% of the
correct results, even when I increased the number of candidates from
15 up to 300 out of 610 users.

After investigation, I found the root cause was a semantic mismatch.
The durable verifier checks for users where the query item scores
WORST -- bottom-k semantics.  But my filter was selecting users where
the query item scores BEST.  So it was filtering in the exact wrong
direction.

[THE FIX - 20 seconds]

I created a new filter called MinRank that correctly selects users
where the query item has the highest rank from the top -- meaning it
scored poorly for those users.  This aligns with the bottom-k
verification condition.

[FINAL RESULT - 20 seconds]

With MinRank, the hybrid pipeline now achieves 100% recall, prunes
97.5% of users, and runs 3.5 to 3.9 times faster than the full
methods.  The robustness study confirms this holds across different
values of k, tau, c, and L.

   worth highlighting as a contribution.
